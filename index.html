Top Destinations in Egypt

Cairo & Giza

Must-Sees: Pyramids of Giza, Sphinx, Egyptian Museum, Khan el-Khalili Bazaar.

Why Visit? Heart of ancient history and vibrant modern culture.

Sharm El-Sheikh

Must-Sees: Ras Mohammed National Park, Naama Bay, Mount Sinai.

Why Visit? Luxury resorts and world-class diving.

Alexandria

Must-Sees: Bibliotheca Alexandrina, Qaitbay Citadel, Mediterranean Corniche.

Why Visit? Coastal charm with Greco-Roman history.

Marsa Alam

Must-Sees: Elphinstone Reef, Dolphin House, Abu Dabbab Beach, Wadi el-Gemal.

Why Visit? Pristine Red Sea paradise for diving, wildlife, and untouched beaches.

Luxor

Must-Sees: Valley of the Kings, Karnak Temple, Luxor Temple, Hot Air Balloon Rides at Sunrise.

Why Visit? The world's greatest open-air museum – where pharaohs, temples and golden tombs bring ancient Egypt to life!

Aswan

Must-Sees: Philae Temple, Abu Simbel, Nile Felucca Rides, Nubian Villages.

Why Visit? Where the Nile shines brightest – from majestic monuments to colorful Nubian culture and tranquil river islands.

Visit Egypt News

Tourism Developments

 Mon, February 25th 2025

The highly anticipated Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) has officially announced its phased opening schedule, with the first galleries welcoming visitors in February 2024. This monumental project near the Giza Pyramids will showcase over 50,000 artifacts, including Tutankhamun's complete treasure collection for the first time. Meanwhile, the Ministry of Tourism has introduced new quality standards for Nile cruises, requiring all vessels to upgrade their safety systems and environmental protections by mid-2024. These changes come as Egypt prepares to handle an expected 30% increase in tourist arrivals following the global marketing campaign "Visit Egypt: Where History Lives."

Cultural Discoveries & Events

 Thu, March 13th 2025

Archaeologists made international headlines last month with the discovery of a perfectly preserved 4,300-year-old pyramid complex near Luxor, believed to belong to a previously unknown queen of the Sixth Dynasty. The site will remain closed for excavation until late 2024, but virtual reality previews are available at the Luxor Museum. On the cultural calendar, preparations are underway for the spectacular reenactment of the Pharaohs' Golden Parade during its third anniversary this November, featuring enhanced light projections and live performances along the 7km route between the Egyptian Museum and GEM.

Travel Advisories & Practical Information

 Tue, May 20th 2025

Travelers should note significant changes to visa policies, with Egypt now offering visa-on-arrival extensions from one to three months for most Western nationals. However, stricter enforcement of monument photography rules has been implemented, particularly at the Valley of the Kings where tripods and professional equipment now require special permits. Health officials also remind visitors that while COVID-19 restrictions have been lifted, vaccination certificates are still recommended for cruise passengers and those visiting remote oases. The Central Bank has warned about fluctuating exchange rates, advising tourists to use official exchange offices rather than street vendors.

Sustainable Tourism Initiatives

 Sat, April 5th 2025

In response to environmental concerns, the Red Sea Governorate has launched an ambitious "Blue Paradise" program, creating marine protected zones around popular dive sites like Elphinstone Reef. Several hotels in Hurghada have transitioned to solar energy completely, with plans to expand this initiative to Luxor by 2025. The government is also piloting a revolutionary "Plastic-Free Nile" project using innovative waste collection barges between Aswan and Luxor, aiming to eliminate single-use plastics from cruise operations entirely within two years.
